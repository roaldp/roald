# Due Diligence — Agent Playbook

## Objective

Conduct systematic evaluation of shortlisted companies to surface risks, validate claims, and produce a structured DD report that enables confident investment decisions.

## When This Runs

After a company passes initial sourcing screening and a partner has approved moving to DD. The sourcing brief is the input; the DD report is the output.

## Financial Analysis

- **Burn rate validation:** Compare stated monthly burn against bank statements or financial model. Flag if discrepancy exceeds 10%.
- **Runway calculation:** Cash in bank divided by trailing 3-month average burn. Flag if stated runway differs from calculated by more than 2 months.
- **Revenue trajectory:** Chart MRR or ARR over last 12 months. Calculate month-over-month growth rate. Flag if growth is decelerating without explanation.
- **Cap table review:** Identify current ownership split, option pool size, and any unusual terms (liquidation preferences above 1x, ratchets, super pro-rata rights).
- **Use of proceeds:** Map the proposed raise against hiring plan and milestones. Does the math hold?

## Market Research

- **TAM/SAM/SOM:** Validate company's market sizing with independent sources. Use bottom-up sizing, not just top-down analyst reports.
- **Competitive landscape:** Map direct competitors, adjacent players, and potential entrants. Identify each competitor's funding, traction, and differentiation.
- **Regulatory environment:** Identify relevant regulations (REACH, MDR, GDPR, export controls for dual-use tech). Assess compliance status and regulatory risk timeline.
- **Customer discovery:** Speak to 3-5 potential or actual customers. Validate willingness to pay, switching costs, and satisfaction.

## Technical Due Diligence

- **IP assessment:** Review patent portfolio (filed, granted, jurisdictions). Identify freedom-to-operate risks.
- **Technology readiness level:** Assign TRL (1-9) with justification. Cross-check against company claims.
- **Defensibility:** Could a well-resourced competitor replicate this in 2 years? What would it cost?
- **Technical debt:** For software-heavy companies, assess architecture quality, test coverage, and deployment practices.
- **Key person risk:** Is the core technology dependent on one individual? What happens if they leave?

## Reference Checks

- **Founder references:** Speak to 2-3 people who have worked with each founder (former colleagues, co-founders, investors). Ask about integrity, resilience, and coachability.
- **Customer references:** Validate product-market fit claims. Ask about alternatives considered and willingness to recommend.
- **Co-investor sentiment:** If there are existing investors, assess their conviction level and follow-on intentions.
- **Back-channel:** At least one reference per founder that was not provided by the company.

## Red Flag Patterns

- Runway mismatch: stated runway does not match burn rate and cash position.
- Founder conflict signals: divergent visions expressed in separate conversations, unusual vesting structures.
- Customer concentration: single customer accounts for more than 30% of revenue.
- Regulatory cliff: product requires approval that is more than 12 months away with no interim revenue path.
- Cap table dysfunction: excessive dilution, dead equity, or investor-unfriendly terms from prior rounds.
- Metrics inflation: counting LOIs as revenue, GMV as revenue, or pilot users as paying customers.

Followed by:
- **Overall recommendation:** Proceed / Proceed with conditions / Do not proceed
- **Key risks and mitigants** (top 3)
- **Open questions** requiring partner-level judgment
